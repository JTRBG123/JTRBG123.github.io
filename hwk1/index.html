Quick Facts

  • Cyber security focuses on confidentiality, integrity, and availability.
  • Most incidents start with a human action such as clicking a malicious link.
  • Strong security is layered, not a single tool.

What is Cyber Security?

Cyber security is the practice of reducing risk in digital systems. It blends technology controls (like encryption and access management), human behavior (like safe browsing), and repeatable processes (like audits and incident response). Effective programs focus on protecting data, keeping services available, and detecting abuse quickly.

A helpful way to think about security is the CIA triad: confidentiality means data is only visible to approved users, integrity means data is accurate and unchanged, and availability means systems stay online when needed. The goal is to keep all three in balance.

Modern security also includes privacy, resilience, and compliance. Privacy limits how data is collected and shared. Resilience ensures a business can recover after a disruption. Compliance helps organizations meet legal and industry requirements.

Core Building Blocks

Identity & Access Network Security Endpoint Protection Monitoring & Response Governance & Policy

These blocks work best together. For example, strong identity controls prevent unauthorized logins, while monitoring alerts the team when something slips past defenses.
