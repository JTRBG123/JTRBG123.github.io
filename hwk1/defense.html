Defense in Depth

Layered controls to reduce and contain attacks.

Overview Threats Defense Careers

Key Layers

  • Identity and access controls
  • Network segmentation
  • Endpoint protection
  • Logging and monitoring

Why Layers Matter

No single security control stops every threat. Defense in depth adds multiple checkpoints so that if one layer fails, another can limit the damage. For example, a phishing email might bypass a spam filter, but multi-factor authentication and least-privilege access can still prevent account takeover.

Layers also improve detection. Logging, alerting, and regular audits help teams spot unusual activity quickly and respond before a small incident becomes a major outage.

Practical Controls

Prevent

  • Patch systems regularly
  • Use strong passwords and MFA
  • Encrypt sensitive data

Detect & Recover

  • Centralize logs for review
  • Test backups and restore plans
  • Run incident response drills
