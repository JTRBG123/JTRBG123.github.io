Threats: Phishing & Social Engineering

How attackers manipulate people to gain access.

Common Signals

  • Unexpected links or attachments
  • Urgent requests for passwords or payments
  • Look-alike domains and spoofed sender names

What is Phishing?

Phishing is a social engineering attack where a message pretends to be from a trusted source. The goal is to trick a person into revealing sensitive data or installing malware. Modern phishing often copies real company branding and uses emotional pressure like urgency or fear to push quick decisions.

Variations include smishing (SMS), vishing (voice calls), and spear phishing, which is tailored to a specific person or team. Attackers often research targets on social media to make messages feel legitimate.

Business email compromise (BEC) is a related tactic where an attacker impersonates a manager or vendor to request wire transfers or sensitive documents. Because BEC relies on deception more than malware, it can bypass traditional security tools.

How to Reduce Risk

  • Verify requests for money or credentials using a second channel.
  • Inspect the full sender address, not just the display name.
  • Use multi-factor authentication to limit the damage of stolen passwords.
  • Report suspicious messages to the security team.
